Group Activities

The Daily Cadence

A daily programme of activities with something for everyone.

Beyond your retreat programme, the Daily Cadence is a schedule of classes and elective wellness activities offered throughout the day. Try something new, explore and discover; all classes are tailored to accommodate different abilities, and classes that are 'advanced' will be so indicated.

Sessions run from morning sunrise movement to sound meditation after dark. Choose those that interest you. Some classes have limited availability, so please reserve a place in advance to ensure your slot.

Most classes are complimentary and included in your retreat. Where there is a separate charge, the fee will be clearly indicated prior to booking.

A group class, mid-morning

There is a sense of community that forms naturally in a group class: people moving and breathing together, sharing an experience, often discovering that the person beside them is on a remarkably similar journey.

We don't run on the clock

The classes rotate and the instructors sometimes change, but what stays steady is the structure of the day: a morning movement window, a midday stretch that anchors the schedule, an afternoon strength block, and an evening practice that brings everything to a close. The Adults Schedule

The adult day has windows, each with its own intention. It opens with movement at sunrise, moves into aqua mid-morning, and settles into a stretch class at midday that runs every day without exception. The afternoon shifts to strength and high-intensity work, the early evening to breath and meditation, and the day closes with something quieter, from candlelight meditation to a sleep class or stargazing from the wellness plateau.

Morning movement at sunrise

Morning

Morning Movement

Mat Pilates · Morning Yoga Flow · Yin Yoga · Tai Chi

There is something particular about moving with other people before the day properly starts, a shared quiet and a collective intention that carries differently than a solo session. The morning chooses which one it needs.

Aqua on the pool deck

Mid-morning

Aqua

Aqua with Pool Noodle · Aqua Hand Buoy · Aqua Boxing

Each session works with water as resistance. Aqua Boxing is the most demanding; the others are accessible at any fitness level and bring a shared lightness that tends to make these one of the more enjoyable hours of the week.

Midday stretch class

Midday

Stretch

Stretch Class · every day · midday

The one fixed point in a week that moves around everything else. It becomes a ritual rather than a class, a daily gathering in the middle of the day before everyone disperses to their afternoons.

Strength and HIIT block

Afternoon

Strength & HIIT

HIIT · TRX · Tabata Training · Circuit Training · Zumba · Step Aerobics · Boot Camp

The most physically demanding window in the day. There is a particular energy to a group strength session; the instructor reads the room at the start and calibrates from there.

Breath and meditation at dusk

Early evening

Breath & Meditation

Pranayama · Singing Bowl Meditation · Floating Meditation · Aerial Yoga

The shift between the active part of the day and its quieter close. Singing bowls sound different in a room full of people breathing slowly together than they do through headphones.

Closing the day under the stars

After dark

Closing the Day

Candle-light Meditation · Better Sleep Class · Star Gazing

Unhurried, quiet, designed to bring the nervous system down. Stargazing on the Red Sea coast is worth doing at least once.

The Children's Schedule

Children have their own programme managed by our Family team, with daily slots across four age bands in the Growing Studios. Every afternoon at four o'clock, the calendar pauses on age bands and runs a Family session instead, bringing parents and children together.

Seed Studio, parent and child

Ages 0 to 3

Seed Studio

Sessions here are parent-paired - sensory play, calm rhythms, gentle exploration of sound and texture. Structured for the particular closeness that happens when a parent and small child discover something together, quietly, without anywhere else to be.

Sprout Studio, gardens and pool

Ages 4 to 8

Sprout Studio

Movement, music, and small-group exploration across the gardens, studios, and pool. A garden scavenger hunt one morning, yoga and animal poses the next, a sound circle after that. At this age, doing things alongside other children in a place this beautiful tends to land as pure adventure.

Stem Lounge, challenges and painting

Ages 9 to 12

Stem Lounge

Sessions here ask more - rhythm mapping, badminton technique, nature relay challenges, dot painting, ocean-themed problem solving. Physical, creative, cognitive, and environmental skills, most designed to work better with a group.

Blossom Lounge, studio and VR

Ages 13+

Blossom Lounge

Longer-format sessions for older children - a kintsugi mindset workshop on resilience, eco house design using recycled materials, underwater memory challenges, strategy-based treasure hunts. Studio space and VR treadmills sit alongside the outdoor programme.

Family slot, everyone together

Every afternoon

Family Slot

Parents and children together · everyone welcome

Every day at four, the age bands dissolve and the whole family does something together: mindful balance relays, rhythm rituals, kindness games, family mini-Olympics. The point is connection rather than instruction.

Pricing and fees

Most activities are complimentary and included in your retreat. Where a charge applies, it is noted in the Daily Cadence and advised on reservation.

Private options

Most group classes can also be arranged as private one-on-one, couple, or family sessions; contact your Wellness Advisor.

Where activities happen

Adults practise across the Health and Wellness Centre, the gardens, the wellness plateau, and the pool deck; some sessions move outside depending on the weather and the instructor. The children's programme runs through the four Growing Studios (Seed, Sprout, Stem, and Blossom Lounge), plus Buttercup Play Space, Pool Play, and Sand Play on the Family side. Watersports and beach sessions launch from the Beach Hut. The venue for each activity is shown on the Daily Cadence.

Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What group activities are available at Jayasom AMAALA?

The Daily Cadence shows the programme of activities for the day and the week. There are dozens of different activities - including fitness, meditative, creative, educational and sustainability actions - something for everyone.

Do I need to book the classes?

Booking is not required unless there is a payable fee or a ceiling on the number of participants. The Daily Cadence will indicate if a booking is required.

Can children join adult classes?

Some specified classes are intended for adults and children together. Outside that window, adults practise in the adult programme and children practise in age-appropriate sessions in the Growing Studios. Some Visiting Practitioners run cross-age workshops when they are in residence.

Are sessions adapted for beginners?

Yes. The instructor reads the room at the start of each session and adjusts accordingly. If you are new to a practice, mention it before the class begins. Sessions that require prior experience will be so indicated.
