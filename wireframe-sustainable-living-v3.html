Sustainable and Regenerative Living

True personal wellbeing depends on a healthy planet.

The Practice

That is the difference between sustainability and regeneration. One is a policy. The other is a practice.

How we live leaves a mark — the pace of it, the consumption, the slow disconnection from what sustains us. We feel the cost in the body long before we read it in the environment.

The Red Sea holds some of the oldest, most resilient corals on earth, adapted to heat over twelve thousand years, with an evolutionary memory no other reef has developed. They can survive the heat stress that would bleach any other system. What they are far less able to survive is what reaches the coast beside them.

Jayasom was built with a clear intention: not to minimise damage, but to actively improve the ecosystem it sits within — the reef, the soil, the water table, the community, and the guest who arrives depleted and leaves carrying something they did not bring.

Our commitment

Sustainability as a functional practice

Here, sustainability is a working practice rather than an aspiration. The decisions that shape a stay — how we build, where energy comes from, how food is grown, and how people move around the property — each carry the same regenerative intent.

Why it matters

Personal health requires environmental wellness.

The MOSO Hub

The MOSO Hub is the operational heart of that belief — the room where sustainability is measured, tracked, and reported in the open throughout the year. It is both a place to learn and a standing commitment to transparency.

Everyday Practice

Water and Sourcing

Minimise Single-Use Items

Water flasks

Complimentary re-usable water flasks are available for each guest, with refill stations readily available. This ensures that single-use bottles are eliminated.

Hydration

Water bottling

At Jayasom AMAALA we bottle our own water in still and sparkling varieties — so we reduce imports and transportation. A splash of lemon with water in the morning is a refreshing way to start the day.

Procurement

Responsible Sourcing

From recycled paper through to the bathroom amenities — which are ethically and organically sourced — we choose sustainability.

The Built Environment

Built to Last Lightly

Design & Build

Sited to the climate

Buildings are positioned to catch the sea breeze and the shade. The architecture sits low against the landscape, built from local, tactile materials, with LEED-certified design throughout.

Coco-Mat™ Mattress

Natural where possible

Mattresses are made from natural fibres — cotton, coconut, natural rubber and seaweed — with no steel or springs. The materials lower allergy and respiratory risk while carrying a lighter environmental cost.

Land & Water

Grown and conserved

The Farm Belt grows in raised, partially-shaded beds that supply the kitchens. Water is conserved and recycled across the property, and stormwater is slowed for gradual drainage rather than runoff.

Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the property carbon neutral?

Jayasom AMAALA operates to a net-zero carbon standard. Energy comes entirely from a dedicated solar farm, and all transport is electric. Performance is tracked and publicly reported each year.

How does Jayasom protect the Red Sea coastline?

Environmental Lighting Zones preserve natural darkness along the coast. Coastal-facing light is directed inward, preserving the navigational cues that nesting turtles rely on. No pesticides are used anywhere on the property.

What is the Farm Belt?

A working growing space of raised beds with partial shade and no pesticides. It feeds the kitchens directly, and local supply is prioritised before anything is sourced externally.

Are products and amenities sustainably sourced?

Yes. Bathroom amenities are ethically and organically sourced, paper is recycled throughout, and responsible sourcing is applied with equal care to every element of a stay.

How do guests get around the property?

Every vehicle is electric — guest transfers and daily logistics alike. Zero-emission transport is standard, not optional.
