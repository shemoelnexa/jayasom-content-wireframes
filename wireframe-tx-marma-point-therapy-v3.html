Marma Point Therapy

The hundred and seven points the texts hold sacred.

Marma Point Therapy is an Ayurvedic session that works one hundred and seven points across the body. The Sanskrit texts describe these as junctions where matter and consciousness meet. The practitioner moves through the points with measured pressure, oil and slow rhythm; the work releases held charge along the energy channels Ayurveda calls nadis.

Description

What Marma Point Therapy is

What it is

The 107 marma points are mapped across the limbs, torso, neck and head. Each is a confluence of muscle, vein, ligament, bone or joint with what the texts describe as a node of life energy. The practitioner reads where charge has built; the session works those points with circular pressure, warm oil and silence.

Why

Marma work is used where ordinary massage cannot reach. The points sit at the meeting of the physical and the energetic; activating them is read in Ayurveda as restoring flow rather than treating a muscle. Guests who carry old injuries, post-surgical guarding or long-running emotional charge often find the session reaches what other bodywork has missed.

107

Mapped points across the body

How it feels

The pressure is firm but not deep. The room is quiet; the work is precise rather than flowing. Some points feel sharp on first contact, then soften; some go warm; some bring an unexpected breath. Most guests are quieter for the rest of the day after a Marma session.

Marma diagram, lineage chart

Method

How the session works

Therapeutic Pillar: Ayurveda, body-mind-spirit axis. The practitioner maps the 107 points by lineage; not every point is worked in every session. Pressure, sequence and oil selection are calibrated to the guest's constitution and the brief. The session is precise rather than flowing; expect stillness, not continuous movement.

Outcomes

What guests notice afterwards

After the session

  • Old guarding patterns released around prior injuries
  • A nervous system that lands lower than ordinary massage takes it
  • Sleep that goes deeper for the next two nights
  • A clearer sense of where the body holds load
  • Better range through joints that have stiffened with age or strain

Common questions

Frequently asked

What is Marma Point Therapy?

An Ayurvedic session that works 107 mapped energy points across the body using calibrated pressure and warm oil; it is read in Ayurveda as restoring flow along the nadis.

Is it the same as acupressure?

They share the idea of mapped points but the lineage is different. Marma comes from Ayurveda; acupressure comes from Traditional Chinese Medicine. Both are offered at Jayasom; the Wellness Advisor routes by brief.

Will it hurt?

The pressure is firm at some points and soft at others. A few points can feel sharp on first contact, then release. Brief any acute pain at the read so the practitioner adapts.

Can I have it during pregnancy?

Several marma points are not worked in pregnancy. The practitioner adapts the session for prenatal guests; brief at the Wellness Advisor read.

How often should I take a session across a stay?

Two or three over a 7 night stay is common; the practitioner sets the cadence at the brief. The work compounds across sessions.
