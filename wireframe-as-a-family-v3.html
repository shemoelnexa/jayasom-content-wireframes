The approach

How the family programme works

A family-attachment retreat with concurrent programmes for parents and young people, designed to nurture the relationships that matter most. Time held to fortify further as a family, in love, trust, and mutual respect. Each member is guided to recognise unique strengths, fostering improved communication, teamwork, and accountability.

The work moves through enriching group and individual experiences in a fun and relaxed environment. The programme blends Family Wellness mentoring with mind-body practices, fitness training, and physiotherapy, alongside shared activities including nature experiences, cooking classes, Spa and holistic rituals, and play-based fitness for children.

A Wellness Advisor holds the day-to-day for the family across ages: shaping the parents' track, guiding the children's daily activities by age band, and threading shared family time through each day at the universal late-afternoon slot. Multi-generational programming is one of the property's core categories rather than a side option, with wellness journeys tailored to move with the family across generations and cultures.

The methodology integrates Traditional Arabic medicine with contemporary clinical science. Children's programmes are wellness-based, educational, and recreational; some sessions incorporate psychology-informed approaches (Cognitive Behavioural Therapy, Neuro-Linguistic Programming, HeartMath) to support emotional, social, and personal skill development. The work does not constitute medical, psychological, psychiatric, or therapeutic treatment, and is not intended to diagnose, treat, or cure any condition. Tech-Integrated Wellness sits within the children's programme as a conscious tool: virtual reality treadmills in the teen lounge, ICAROS interactive fitness, MultiBall projected sport, and structured digital experiences run alongside hands-on play.

The retreat is calibrated for families seeking calm, considered family time. The programme is purposeful rather than loud, designed to hold space for elders, parents, and children of every age band without overstimulation or generic family-resort patterns.

The setting

Jayela, the family side of the property

Jayasom AMAALA stands on Saudi Arabia's Red Sea coast, part of the Triple Bay master plan developed by Red Sea Global. The property is set low against the coastline, with cross-ventilation as the first cooling layer and locally sourced materials throughout.

For As a Family guests, the relevant zone is Jayela, the family side of the property, with shared programmes and family Mediterranean dining. Several venues thread the family experience together, with cross-zone access extending the experience into the gardens and the wellness plateau beyond Jayela alone.

Three Buttercup play spaces

Buttercup Playground · Pool Play · Sand Play

Three play spaces carry the Buttercup name. Buttercup Playground sits near the retail village, partially shaded, with wood materials and a sandy floor; climbing features and slides run for the middle ages, balance hammocks reach the older children. Buttercup Pool Play is next to the children's pool, in the shade of the Jayela Terraces; the walls carry bouldering grips and the rubberised floor features partial domes for running and rolling. Buttercup Sand Play sits towards the cove coastline, with shaded climbing structures, a slide, tunnels, and a pathway to the beach front for strollers and mobility equipment.

Family Pools, Beach Hut, and outdoor

From the pool deck to the cove

The Jayela Family Pools sit on the ground floor of the Jayela Terraces. Two pools, one deeper and one shallow at zero to forty centimetres for young children. Sofa seating and changing rooms are at hand, with toilets under the shade of the building.

The Beach Hut launches non-motorised watersports: stand-up paddleboards, snorkelling, swim lessons, and water-borne yoga. Rinsing showers are available. Beyond the Beach Hut, the Red Sea cove opens onto kayaking, scuba diving, kitesurfing, and boat excursions. The Corallium offers an immersive learning experience focused on the Red Sea's coral reefs and marine life. Day trips into the Prince Mohammed bin Salman Royal Reserve add geological history and cultural exploration; conservation and marine biological tours run alongside.

Active Courts and Family Hydrothermal

The Jayela Health & Wellness Centre

The Jayela Health & Wellness Centre houses the Active Courts, lit at night for extended hours. Trampolines run at ground level for individual use; a bouldering wall sits on a rubberised landing surface; Fuwa Domes hold inflated structures for climbing and sliding; a covered Multi-use Games Court (MUGA) carries volleyball, badminton, and other family or community sports.

A Family Hydrothermal experience moves through seven stages at the family's pace: infrared sauna for heat activation, waterfall bathing, Kneipp walking, vitality pools at varying temperatures and mineral compositions, steam, Rasul rituals, and a flotation pool.

Family Rooms

Calibrated for size and cohesion

Family rooms are calibrated for size and cohesion. Single and multi-bedroom pool villas sit alongside the Terrace rooms, conveniently located near the family pools and offering stunning seaside views. The largest family accommodation, the 5-bedroom Jayela Residence, comes complete with its own private wellness studio. Selected accessible rooms within the Jayela Terraces remove steps and add electrically assisted beds, vibrating and lighted fire alarms, emergency call systems, easy-access shower and toilets, and grab rails.

Programme inclusions

What every retreat carries

Every retreat tier carries a baseline of inclusions across stays. Three healthy cuisine meals per night come through Loumi Rue and the family-side venues, with children's menus designed for the family-dining concept. The Family Hydrothermal sits within reach as a separate experience from the adult Jayana circuit, including the seven-stage circuit and the Family Wellness Private Scrub Room.

Daily programming runs concurrently for parents and children. The Adults rotation holds yoga, Mat Pilates, breath-led practice, and aqua sessions for adult-track guests on top of the family hour. The children's day moves through five 40-minute slots calibrated by the four Growing Studios. Both tracks meet at the universal family hour each afternoon, four to five, every day of the stay.

One cooking class at Nakeha Studio comes with every stay from the four-night Explore tier upwards. A daily Choice of Spa, also from the Explore tier, pairs a sixty-minute adult treatment with a thirty-minute child treatment for guests under twelve.

Per-tier Family treatment counts are set out above in the stay structure: eleven at Explore, eighteen at Absorb, twenty-four at Immerse, thirty-four at Commit.

Skill Development

Activities across the rotation are tagged across thirty Skill Development categories, including emotional development, sensory awareness, cognitive skills, fine and gross motor skills, language development, family connection, problem solving, and environmental awareness. Residences pathway

For longer stays and multi-suite bookings

For families considering longer stays or multi-suite bookings, Jayasom Wellness Residences offer a residential extension of the retreat. The 24-villa community sits across ten two-bedroom, twelve three-bedroom, and two four-bedroom villas, alongside the 5-bedroom Jayela Residence with its private wellness studio for the largest family stays.

Residence owners receive the Longevity Assessment Programme annually across seven components, alongside private wellness programming that runs year-round. For multigenerational families and intergenerational bookings, the Residences pathway opens longer rhythms than a four-to-fourteen-night retreat allows.

Beyond the seven-night canonical tier

How the rhythm flexes

Four-night Explore compresses the family rhythm; ten- and fourteen-night Immerse and Commit expand the calendar. The three Family-recommended add-ons (Free We Learn, Rehear The Heartbeats, Restore & Preserve) layer in across longer stays. The Wellness Advisor walks the brief through with you in the pre-arrival call; the Wellness Residences pathway opens for families wanting longer cycles than the wellness destination tiers hold.

Jayasom AMAALA's As a Family retreat sits within the Triple Bay master plan on Saudi Arabia's Red Sea coast. Three to fourteen nights, with concurrent tracks for adults and children across age-banded studios, shared family hours each afternoon, and family-tailored treatments, bodywork, and dining. The Wellness Advisor holds the programme from the pre-arrival conversation through to closing.
