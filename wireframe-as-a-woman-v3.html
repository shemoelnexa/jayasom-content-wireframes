Featured treatments

Eight rituals for the work

Eight named treatments anchor the As a Woman programme. Each is paced to where you are; each is ordered by your Wellness Advisor at the pre-arrival call and adjusted as the stay unfolds.

The Balancing Ritual

The Balancing Ritual

Duration: 90 minutes

A whole-body approach to hormonal change. Aromatherapy foot baths, breathwork, Cold Stone Therapy, and Lymphatic Drainage work together to ease the physical signature of perimenopause and menopause and bring the body back into balance. The hero ritual at the centre of the work.

Shirodhara

Shirodhara

Duration: 90 minutes

The classic Ayurvedic Mind Calm. Warm medicated oil pours in a continuous stream over the third-eye point on the forehead, dissolving mental chatter and easing nervous-system load. Frequently chosen by women navigating sleep disruption and anxious thinking through hormonal shifts.

NEW Mum, Blessing of Becoming

NEW Mum, Blessing of Becoming

Duration: 50 or 90 minutes

A restorative postnatal ritual that honours the transition into motherhood. Gentle realignment, soothing of tired muscles, and the emotional holding new mothers need.

The Comforting Ritual (Pregnancy)

The Comforting Ritual (Pregnancy)

Duration: 50 or 90 minutes

Designed specifically for prenatal recovery. Hands-on therapy releases the physical strains of pregnancy while creating a calm, safe space for the mother-to-be to settle into the body's changes.

Marma Point Therapy

Marma Point Therapy

Duration: 50 minutes

The vitality booster. A focused energy treatment targeting one hundred and seven Marma points, the junctions where matter and consciousness meet in Ayurvedic teaching. Applied with gentle pressure and essential oils, it works the points connected to hormonal and energetic balance.

Sound Healing Therapy

Sound Healing Therapy

Duration: 60 minutes

Singing bowls, gongs, and chimes work together at the Sonorium. Vibrations move through the body, promoting deep relaxation and helping the nervous system settle. Frequently selected by women pairing the treatment with a Pranayama or Meditation slot in the same day.

Detoxifying Balneotherapy

Detoxifying Balneotherapy

Duration: 30 minutes

Underwater jets paired with essential oils stimulate circulation, boost lymphatic flow, and support the body's detoxification pathways. The treatment draws on an ancient ritual of meeting the body with the power of the ocean.

Watsu

Watsu

Duration: 60 minutes

Passive water therapy in a warm pool. The therapist gently stretches and mobilises the body to release tension, ease discomfort, and restore the breath. Particularly relevant for women carrying chronic muscular tension or recovering from a stressful season.

The methodology

The approach

A methodology anchored in regional heritage

The work draws on the Wellness Wisdom® pillar of the Jayasom system. Beneath that sits the Ground Wellbeing practice, the named treatment system through which the work happens. Threaded through both is Traditional Arabic Medicine (TAIM, also known as Tibb), the regional heritage that anchors Jayasom's clinical approach in the Kingdom. The methodology recognises that a woman's body changes across the seasons it moves through; the same body needs different support at each.

Hormonal and life-stage assessment opens every stay, run as a one-to-one consultation with the resident-clinician on day one and revisited at the closing conversation. Around that assessment, the daily rhythm of bodywork, movement, and rest is calibrated to where you are now and the energy you arrive with. Cultural fluency runs through the property: privacy and cultural respect operate as daily standards, with gender sensitivity built into how the timetable is shaped.

The setting

A property sized for the practice

Jayasom AMAALA sits within the Triple Bay master plan on Saudi Arabia's Red Sea coast. The property holds sixty villas plus twenty-four private residences. Phase one is intentionally small by wellness destination scale; the daily rhythm is sized to the cohort, not to the building. Triple Bay is part of the AMAALA destination, a regional flagship for women's wellness in the Kingdom.

Yasmeen Night Garden runs in scent-only mode through the evening hours, designed for slow walking. Our Stonecrop Maze sits on the Wellness Plateau and is open through the late afternoon. The Phoenix Garden holds private daytime rest and Sound Healing Therapy runs at the Sonorium. Within the Jayana zone, the As a Woman programme operates with adult-side privacy and dedicated quiet hours.

Cultural fluency

Privacy and respect, operationally

Daily standards

Cultural fluency runs through the property. Staff training covers Saudi etiquette and respectful discretion, with gender sensitivity built in across the day. Photography is at the guest's discretion. Private entrances and protected schedules are available where requested. Communications stay private through the booking flow; credit-card statements use a discreet entity name; family-shared accounts can be replaced with a private channel for the booking. Women-only therapist preferences are honoured, and women-only timetable blocks for movement and bodywork are scheduled daily during As a Woman cohorts.

Programme by life stage

Three named tracks

The programme adjusts to the season you arrive in. Three named tracks hold the structure; your Wellness Advisor selects the right combination at the pre-arrival conversation.

Track 01

Perimenopause and menopause

The Balancing Ritual sits at the centre. Shirodhara, Marma Point Therapy, and Therapeutic Lymphatic Drainage support the hormonal layer; Cooling protocols and the Detoxifying Balneotherapy address heat and water retention. Nutrition consultations anchor on the Women's Hormonal Health menu (maca root, avocado, pumpkin seed, flax seeds, green tea, and supporting whole foods designed to stabilise hormones, energy, and mood). Movement runs lighter: Mat Pilates, Hatha Yoga, Pranayama, and aqua sessions for low-impact strengthening.

Track 02

Postnatal and prenatal

NEW Mum, Blessing of Becoming and the Comforting Ritual (Pregnancy) anchor the bodywork. The track calibrates around the mother's recovery window; postnatal stays from six weeks onwards are supported with gentle realignment, while prenatal stays follow strict protocols cleared with the resident-clinician. Sleep Ritual sessions support broken-sleep patterns. The Wellness Advisor coordinates with your physician where required.

Track 03

General wellness and reset

For women not in a specific life-stage transition but seeking a deep reset: stress recovery, sleep recalibration, fitness reset, or aesthetic wellness. The Vagus Nerve Ritual anchors stress-system work; the Sleep Ritual addresses insomnia; HIFU, Cristal Fit, and the Hydrafacial Collection sit on the aesthetic side; Personal Training and Yoga structure the movement layer.

Programme inclusions

What every retreat carries

The Hydrothermal facilities are accessible through the day, including infrared sauna for heat activation, vitality pools at varying temperatures, steam, and the flotation pool. Daily fitness and movement classes run from morning to evening; women-led classes are available where requested.

Beyond the seven-night canonical tier

How the rhythm flexes

Compression to four-night Explore (10 named treatments) and expansion to ten-night Immerse (26) and fourteen-night Commit (35); track variation widens; the Wellness Mentoring Consultation Women and Cooking classes at Nakeha sit tier-gated.

In residence

Practitioners in Residence

Resident-clinicians hold the medical and clinical layer of the programme. Hormonal and wellness assessments are run by the resident-clinician on day one and revisited at the closing conversation. Diagnostics available to the assessment include blood-panel testing, food-intolerance testing, and Heart-Focused Breathing and Biofeedback (a heart-rate variability protocol that informs nervous-system pacing across the stay).

Three named consultation pathways sit alongside the standard assessment: Traditional Arabic and Islamic Medicine (TAIM, drawn from the regional heritage); Naturopathic; and Ayurvedic. Each pathway is matched to where you are and the questions you arrive carrying.

The Wellness Advisor holds the programme across the consultation team, from pre-arrival through to closing. Visiting Practitioners rotate in season for specialist tracks: integrative therapists, somatic practitioners, embodied-practice teachers. The roster shifts; check the current rotation at the pre-arrival call. Women-only therapist preferences are honoured.

Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best women's wellness retreat in Saudi Arabia?

Jayasom AMAALA's As a Woman retreat sits on the Red Sea coast within the Triple Bay master plan, integrating Traditional Arabic Medicine with contemporary clinical science. It runs four to fourteen nights and is shaped at arrival by your Wellness Advisor. Three programme tracks anchor the work: perimenopause and menopause; postnatal and prenatal recovery; including a general wellness reset.

How long should I stay for a women's wellness retreat?

Stays run from four to fourteen nights, adaptable to your schedule and intent. The methodology names four tiers: four nights to Explore, seven nights to Absorb, ten nights to Immerse, fourteen nights to Commit. Your Wellness Advisor will recommend a duration during the pre-arrival conversation, calibrated to the life-stage track you choose.

Can I travel alone, or do I need to bring a companion?

Solo women travellers are common at As a Woman; the property is calibrated for solo, paired, and small-group bookings. Privacy and cultural sensitivity run through the design; discretion is operational. Some women travel with a sister or close friend; others choose the Residences with a small group.

What's the cultural environment like for women guests from the GCC and Saudi Arabia?

Privacy and respectful discretion are operational standards; gender sensitivity is built into the daily timetable. Photography is at the guest's discretion. Private entrances and protected schedules are available where requested; women-only therapist preferences are honoured. The Traditional Arabic and Islamic Medicine consultation runs as a standard pathway for women who want a heritage-rooted lens on their care.

Can I bring my mother or daughter on the retreat as well?

Yes. As a Woman accommodates multigenerational women's retreats well; bookings can be coordinated through the Wellness Residences pathway with your Wellness Advisor holding the programme across the group. For families with children present, the As a Family retreat is the cohort-fit option.

Walking out

You leave with a clearer brief, a tier match for next time, and the Wellness Advisor reachable for follow-up questions. The take-home routines (nutrition guidance, breathwork, sleep tools) are written down before checkout. The relationship continues.
