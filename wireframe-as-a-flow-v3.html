Featured Treatments

Six experiences anchor the As a Flow retreat across active recovery, mobility, and rebuilding. Your Wellness Advisor places each against the load the body is carrying and the goals you arrived with.

Most guests arrive carrying a load. The week takes that as the starting point. Days build around the body's recovery curve, not a fixed grid. Some sessions move with you, some are placed where the practitioner thinks they'll matter most. What doesn't move: Stretch Class at eleven, the conditioning slot at four, the hydrothermal circuit whenever you want it. Treatment counts depend on how long you stay. Eleven at four nights. Eighteen at seven. Twenty-six at ten. Thirty-five at fourteen, and the rehabilitation work going deeper across the fortnight.

Muscle Recovery Ritual

Muscle Recovery Ritual

Duration: 90 or 120 minutes

The hero ritual of As a Flow. Targeted release of deep-seated tension and metabolic waste from the muscles. Built for athletes and guests carrying high-intensity training loads.

Therapeutic Lymphatic Drainage

Therapeutic Lymphatic Drainage

Gentle hands-on work that clears blockages through rhythmic movements, activating lymph glands and pumping fluid through the channels. Recovery-arc territory; supports post-load fluid clearance and reduced inflammation. Sits in longer stays.

Neurac® Method

Neurac® Method (Neuromuscular Activation)

Duration: 50 minutes

A sling-based system. Three-dimensional suspension therapy that re-educates pain-free movement patterns for acute or chronic conditions, restoring muscle function through specialised physiotherapy techniques. Where chronic pain or post-injury rebuilds need depth, this is the work.

Gyrotonic®

Gyrotonic®

Duration: 50 minutes

Pulley-based three-hundred-and-sixty-degree movement. The spine and limbs stretch and strengthen at once; flexibility and mobility build alongside. Length, range, recovery, woven into a strength-led week.

Watsu

Watsu

Active recovery without active effort. Passive water therapy in a warm pool, drawn from Zen Shiatsu; the therapist stretches and mobilises the body, releasing tension and easing the breath back.

Cryotherapy

Cryotherapy

Whole-body exposure to extremely low temperatures, brief and bracing. Inflammation drops, post-training recovery accelerates, metabolism gets a useful nudge.

Overview

An Active Way to Recover

The As a Flow retreat is set around what the body needs to do across the stay. The retreat is for athletes and fitness enthusiasts. Guests carrying chronic pain or injuries who need guidance on posture, soft-tissue mobilisation, or rehabilitation. People rebuilding from a long stretch of sedentary work.

The methodology your Wellness Advisor draws on names three goals for the work:

Three goals

  • Improve fitness or sporting performance.
  • Promote rehab from prior injury.
  • Explore new activities for the first time that may turn out to be your new favourite.

Your Advisor weighs all three against what arrives on the day, and the programme settles around the one that matters most. The methodology rests on the Wellness Wisdom® pillar that runs through every Jayasom programme: traditional wisdom met by contemporary practice, with Traditional Arabic Medicine integrated alongside sport science, physiotherapy, and the daily clinical work of the Health and Wellness Centre. The work is precise, measurable, and answerable to the body in front of us.

The eleven o'clock Stretch Class anchors the morning, the four o'clock high-intensity block defines the afternoon, and recovery work threads between.

Morning at the studio

Daily rhythm

Your Days at As a Flow

A day takes shape the morning you arrive. Your Advisor sits down with you, reads the questionnaire you sent in the weeks before, and asks where the body is today. Current training load, recent injury or surgery, the goals you arrived carrying. The questions reach further than a standard arrival intake; what you say on the day sets the week.

Seven in the morning, the studio opens. Mat Pilates, Morning Yoga Flow, Yin Yoga, and Tai Chi rotate by day, paired to where the body is. By eleven, everyone is on the mat for the Stretch Class; it runs every day of the week and most guests come to think of it as the spine of the morning. Breakfast at Saratô follows. Mint and perilla anchor the kitchen, hyperlocal and hyperfresh; all dishes gluten-free; the appetite turns up after the studio.

The afternoon belongs to the four o'clock conditioning slot. HIIT, TRX, Tabata Training, Circuit Training, Zumba, Step Aerobics, and Boot Camp rotate Sunday to Saturday; high-intensity, bounded. Seven-night stays add Personal Training, with one-to-one coaching shaping load against the goals from arrival. Between blocks, Chia Bite near the Health and Wellness Centre handles the refuel: power juice, nutrient-dense bites, the tea programme. Five minutes is enough.

Aqua classes run in the cool of the mineral pool at ten o'clock daily. Pool Noodle, Hand Buoy, and Boxing variants rotate so the body works without the joint load of the studio floor.

By six the body wants stillness. The six o'clock reflective slot offers Pranayama, Singing Bowl Meditation, Floating Meditation, or Aerial Yoga. The hydrothermal circuit runs alongside through the evening: infrared sauna, waterfall bathing, Kneipp walking, vitality pools at varying temperatures, steam, Rasul ritual, flotation pool. Use any sequence the body wants. Dinner runs at Saratô or at Jiraka (beef-free, the spice-route specialty kitchen); the kitchen minimises gluten, refined sugars, salt, and coffee, and reaches first for what the Farm Belt grows. The eight o'clock evening slot holds Better Sleep Class, Star Gazing, or Candlelight Meditation.

Three meals each day from the kitchen, beginning with dinner on arrival. The room is set up around your stated preferences: aroma options of Calming Lavender or Spirited Rosemary, a chosen pillow from the seven on the menu, a Coco-mat™ natural mattress.

The setting

The Setting at AMAALA

Jayasom AMAALA sits on Saudi Arabia's Red Sea coast, within the Triple Bay master plan being developed by Red Sea Global. The coast holds a year-round outdoor training climate. Saudi Arabia's eVisa programme opens guest entry across a wide range of markets. Buildings recede in the right places and sit low against the coastline; sea breeze moves before mechanical cooling; materials are local where the climate allows them.

For As a Flow guests, the relevant zone is Jayana, the adults retreat area at the southern end of Triple Bay. Movement studios sit within the adult-side wellness building. Mineral and vitality pools support aqua classes and active recovery. The hydrothermal circuit threads infrared sauna, waterfall bathing, Kneipp walking, vitality pools at varying temperatures and mineral compositions, steam, Rasul rituals, and a flotation pool through one progressive sequence.

Outdoor work runs from the Beach Hut on the cove: kayaking, paddleboarding, swim conditioning, and water-borne mobility work between training blocks. The Corallium and the Red Sea reefs sit beyond the Beach Hut for guests adding marine activity. The Prince Mohammed bin Salman Royal Reserve carries hiking and trail running for guests adding distance.

The Jayana adult-side gardens thread through the property and earn their place in the recovery arc. Yasmeen Night Garden, named for the jasmine that opens after dark, sits at the foot of the Wellness Plateau and reads as a wind-down room after the four o'clock conditioning slot. The Wellness Plateau itself is reached by a winding path; the climb tightens the calves and opens the chest, which most athletic guests find a useful evening rebound from a heavy training day. The Stonecrop Maze sits at the plateau's centre, with slow-walking discipline that holds for active recovery work between studio sessions. Phoenix Garden, near Saratô, is an open-air Phoenix dactylifera plantation reservable for evening meals.

The standard accommodation is the One-Bedroom Beachfront Pool Villa: sixty-two square metres indoor and forty-eight square metres outdoor, balcony, lounge chairs, plunge pool, Coco-mat™ natural mattress, Oud-custom amenities. Two-bedroom and three-bedroom pool villas are available; over-water villas face the bay for guests on longer stays. Each room is set up around stated preferences: aroma options (Calming Lavender or Spirited Rosemary), pillow choice from the seven on the menu, and the room held to the property's temperature standard.

Sixty villas plus twenty-four private residences hold the property. Phase one is a small property by destination-resort scale, and the design is for it to stay that way; the daily wellness rhythm is sized to the cohort.

Programme inclusions

What's Included

Stay length shapes the depth of the work. Four tier names hold the structure: four nights to Explore, seven nights to Absorb, ten nights to Immerse, fourteen nights to Commit. Pacing matches the tier; nothing across the stay is added because of the calendar.

Every As a Flow stay includes

  • Accommodation in your chosen room category, with daily housekeeping
  • Three wellness cuisine meals per night, beginning with dinner on the day of arrival
  • Wellness minibar in your room, stocked according to the Specialist Consultation
  • Individual health and wellness consultation with your Wellness Advisor on arrival, revisited at the closing conversation
  • Specialist Consultation (50 min, all tiers)
  • Physical and Postural Assessment plus VISIA Facial Imaging, complimentary on request
  • Daily fitness and leisure activities across the property's twenty-two-class adult roster
  • Unlimited use of all wellness destination facilities (steam, sauna, mineral and vitality pools, salt inhalation rooms, infrared rooms, mixed gym, hydrothermal circuit)
  • One Daily Treatment per night of stay from the seven-treatment menu (Jayasom Signature, Balinese, Deep Tissue, Classical Swedish, Chakra Balancing, Foot Reflexology, Abhyanga)
  • The Jayasom Collection (welcome amenity)

Personalisation note

Programme inclusions are indicative; alternatives are available; your Wellness Advisor finalises the programme shortly after arrival, around your individual needs and stated objectives.

Services and facilities are subject to government health regulations.

For active guests

Sessions and Rituals for Active Recovery

Across the As a Flow retreat, several treatments and sessions are reserved for athletes, active guests, and those rebuilding after injury. The body that arrives, the goals from the arrival conversation, and the tier you choose all shape which surface across the stay.

Boot Camp, Pilates, and Yoga thread through every tier. Postural Corrective Therapy and the daily Daily Treatment hold across every tier.

Seven-night Absorb stays add Personal Training, Aqua classes, Super Stretch, and Boxing. The Wellness Advisor weighs each against the load the week is carrying. A Cooking class with the Nakeha Studio chef threads through; the kitchen's research on which foods support which conditions runs continuously, and the class is the way that work meets the guest.

Ten-night Immerse stays bring Therapeutic Lymphatic Drainage and Cryotherapy into the recovery arc. Pranayama adds to the breath-led work, and one Restorative Treatment (the Muscle Recovery Ritual) threads through.

Fourteen-night Commit stays open the rehabilitation depth: Watsu, Gyrotonic®, Neurac® Method, and physiotherapy. Two Restorative Treatments thread through. Practitioner relationships build across the stay; the closing conversation reads the work back across the fortnight.

The eleven o'clock Stretch Class holds the property's morning anchor across every tier and every day.

Sports nutrition

Wellness Through Nutrition

Sports nutrition sits at the centre of the work. The Bespoke Sports Nutrition Specialist Consultation (50 min, all tiers) opens the relationship; from there, the Health and Wellness Advisors guide menu selection across three healing-menu programmes that support the athletic recovery cycle.

Neuro Nourish

Supports cognitive performance during heavy training cycles. Nutrition that affects focus, memory, and mood; omega-3s, antioxidants, and adaptogens supporting mental clarity alongside physical recovery.

Key ingredients: blueberry, walnuts, matcha, cacao.

Gut Healing and Cleansing

Supports guests rebuilding after injury or sustained training load. Probiotics, fibre, and anti-inflammatory foods restore balance.

Key ingredients: kefir, chia seeds, fermented vegetables, turmeric, flax seeds.

Women's Hormonal Health

Available across the kitchen for guests where hormonal balance enters the picture; menus rich in healthy fats, proteins, and key micronutrients support overall vitality.

Key ingredients: maca root, avocado, pumpkin seed, flax seeds, green tea.

The culinary team crafts three-day healing menus across the stay, blending nutritional science with culinary work. Your Wellness Advisor surfaces the programme that matches what the body needs.

In residence

Practitioners in Residence

Wellness Advisors hold the As a Flow programme. Each Advisor combines a clinical background with a sports or movement specialism, and a small caseload at any one time so the programme is set, not delivered. The Advisor meets you on arrival, walks the brief through the days that follow, revisits the work at the closing conversation, and remains reachable after you leave.

Visiting Practitioners arrive at Jayasom in residence, for weeks at a time, drawn from a roster of clinical and movement specialists. Each is chosen for the depth of practice they carry. The teaching that arrives with a Visiting Practitioner stays with the resident team once they have left.

The resident team holds the daily work alongside. Mohammad Almslmany leads the rehabilitation programme as Physiotherapist; his practice integrates evidence-based physiotherapy with fitness and mobility training, anchored in Orthopaedic Manual Therapy, Myofascial Needling Therapy, and ISSA Personal Training certifications, and a belief that recovery is strengthened by trust and conversation alongside the physical work. Fitness specialists run the group-class roster and the one-to-one Personal Training. Recovery technicians manage the hydrothermal circuit, the cryotherapy chamber, and the active-recovery infrastructure.

After departure

Beyond the Stay

The work asked at Jayasom does not finish at checkout. Through any As a Flow stay, the take-home routines (training brief, mobility programme, nutrition guidance, recovery protocols) are written down before checkout. Your Wellness Advisor holds the relationship as the cycle resumes at home, and Visiting Practitioners can be referenced where specialist follow-up helps.

A Wellness Residences pathway runs alongside, for active guests building athletic recovery into a longer cycle than the four-to-fourteen-night retreat tiers hold. Twenty-four villas across three layouts (ten 2-bedroom at 350.2 square metres, twelve 3-bedroom at 423.9 square metres, two 4-bedroom at 525.3 square metres) sit adjacent to Jayasom AMAALA within Triple Bay. The Jayasom Longevity Assessment Programme runs across seven components annually for residence owners, supported by the same Wellness Advisor who shapes the retreat stay; for athletes building training-cycle continuity, the assessment cadence reads as longitudinal performance work.
