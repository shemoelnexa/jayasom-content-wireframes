Jayasom AMAALA

A Heart-Centered Space of Healing

A wellness destination on the northern shore of the Red Sea, where seven thousand square metres of healing facilities open onto the cove and the desert beyond.

Plan Your Stay

Location

Triple Bay, AMAALA

AMAALA is a new destination envisioned as a connected coastal community at Triple Bay. Whilst the northern end hosts a lively Marina Village, the south end, connected by a linear walking route, is home to the more secluded wellness core.

Travel

One hour by car from Al Wajh, two hours from Red Sea International, two hours from NEOM Bay.

The Resort

A purpose-led plan between cove and plateau

Sixty wellness villas and twenty-four wellness residences sit between the cove and the inland Wellness Plateau. The plan is purpose-led: Jayana for adults at the southern end, Jayela for families to the north, the Health and Wellness Centre threaded between them.

The Spa

Care as the foreground

Treatment rooms open onto private gardens. Hydrotherapy, sound healing, breathwork, and movement studios sit alongside clinical-medical assessment rooms. The architecture works with sound and light to make care the foreground.

Spa interior

A Holistic Approach

A Restorative Approach to Wellness

Wellness Wisdom®, Sustainability Pledge, and Power of Place anchor everything we do. Therapeutic Pillars, not modalities, shape your week. The Wellness Advisor builds the path with you.

Hydrotherapy lower terrace

Hydrotherapy

Pools, contrast, and breath

Cold and warm pools, contrast circuits, mineral baths, and Watsu sit at the lower terrace. Sessions are paired with breath and movement work; the order shifts to suit you.

A Sanctuary for the Senses

Designed to heal through architecture and sound

Buildings frame the cove and the wadi; quiet hours are held; rooms feel held rather than branded.

Culinary

Philosophy of Nourishment

Eight venues, one shared discipline. No alcohol, no deep frying, fourteen approved cooking methods, in-house bottled water. The kitchens cook for restoration.

Atmosphere

Quiet Luxury Retreat

Wellness with discretion. Care without performance. The work is small and constant, the kind that compounds across a year.

Landscape

Nature-Framed Living

Five named gardens, three Buttercup play venues, the Stonecrop Maze on the Wellness Plateau. The landscape is a method, not a backdrop.

Restaurants

Eight venues, one shared discipline

Eight venues across the property. Saratô, Jiraka, and Shay Waard on the adult side. Loumi Rue, Chez Mitzuba, and Breezy Kale on the family side. Chia Bite and Nakeha Studio at the Health and Wellness Centre, shared by all.

Kitchen

Philosophy of Nourishment

Six Core Values, five Cornerstones of Wellness Cuisine. The kitchen reads ingredients first, then the plate. Banned-ingredient discipline applies across all eight venues.

Arrival

First Experience

A welcome meal at Saratô on the cove, paired with a pause-led brief from your Wellness Advisor. The week begins at the table.

Activities and Programmes

A schedule that shapes itself

Twenty-two adult classes plus children's programming. Yoga, Pilates, breathwork, sound, water-borne yoga at the Beach Hut, talking circles in the Phoenix Garden. The schedule shapes itself; the team brings what's needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Before you arrive

When does Jayasom AMAALA open?

Phase one opens in 2026. Booking enquiries are taken now; allocation runs against the Wellness Advisor consultation.

How do I get to AMAALA?

Al Wajh airport is one hour by car. Red Sea International and NEOM Bay are each two hours. Airport pickup is included; we'd rather not have guests randomly arriving.

What's the booking process?

An enquiry triggers a Wellness Advisor consultation, then allocation. The Advisor builds your week from what's available that week, not from a fixed schedule.
