Families and Shared Well-being

Wellness for every generation, held together.

Jayasom AMAALA is not only for adults. It is intended for families across generations — parents and children, grandparents and teenagers, the whole family however it arrives. Each has their own dedicated spaces and team. And they unite at the points of the day built for exactly that. The children's experience is as carefully designed as the programme we have curated for the adults.

Jayela — for families

Jayela – For Families

What will my children actually do?

The answer lies in the combination of the natural setting, purpose-designed spaces and activities created for every stage of development.

The coastline and hill trails invite exploration, while sports courts, trampolines, climbing walls and active spaces encourage movement and play. Indoors, creative and sound studios, immersive technology and age-specific areas offer further ways to discover and learn.

Programmes are tailored by age and guided by teams trained to understand children's changing interests and needs. The aim is not simply to fill their days, but to inspire confidence, curiosity and healthy habits that last beyond their stay.

Family Wellness

The whole family. Separate yet together.

A Wellness Advisor listens and helps guide the right mix of activities for the needs of all family members — grandparents, parents and children of all ages. Teenagers of 16 years and above participate in the adult programmes, with parental consent required for some specified activities.

A day might feel like this

Morning Energy

For early risers, the day may begin with a walk along the beach, followed by yoga, stretching or a guided breathing meditation.

Breakfast

Join Loumi Rue and friends for a nourishing breakfast designed to sustain energy and set the tone for the day ahead.

Mid-Morning

Adults may follow their own programme, while children take part in age-specific activities. Across both, the focus may shift between exploration, discovery, movement, creativity and restoration — always with room for learning and laughter.

Lunch

Come back together over lunch, share stories from the morning and decide what the afternoon might hold: more activities, time by the pool, a snooze, or a soak in the hydrothermal mineral pools.

Later Afternoon

A shared family experience brings everyone together, with activities designed for all ages to enjoy — should they wish to join.

Dinner and Beyond

Evenings can be shared or shaped around separate rhythms, depending on what the family needs. The pace gradually softens, helping everyone settle, restore and prepare for a restful night.

Children's Programme

Daily Cadence

Our children's programme is guided by one simple belief: children thrive when their environment is designed around their needs, rather than carved out from the adult world. Throughout each day, a varied schedule of age-specific activities invites children to move, create, explore, connect and find moments of calm.

Digital detox is part of the approach — but the emphasis is not simply on taking screens away. It is on offering richer alternatives: active, creative, social and mindful experiences that capture children's attention naturally, while supporting their physical, emotional, social and cognitive development.

Age-banded

The Four Growing Studios

Four studios are anchored around children of different ages — no-one is too young, through to teenagers who have their own dedicated space. Each studio has indoor and outdoor areas, with outdoor spaces shaded and enclosed. Named for the growing cycle of plants.

Seed Studio

Ages 0 – 3

Seed Studio

The infant stage is the first of all stages — the most fragile, yet the most promising. All is new; all is yet to come. Sessions here are parent-paired: sensory play, calm rhythms, gentle exploration of sound and texture. Closeness that happens when a parent and small child discover something together, quietly, without anywhere else to be.

Sprout Studio

Ages 4 – 8

Sprout Studio

The toddler stage is about striving for independence and autonomy — the time for curiosity and love of exploring the environment. Each day brings a changing mix of movement, music and small-group exploration across the gardens, studios and pool: from animal-inspired movement and sound circles to playful encounters with nature. Shared with other children, every experience feels like a new adventure.

Stem Lounge

Ages 9 – 12

Stem Lounge

The tween years mark a transitional period between childhood and adolescence. Sessions here ask more of participants — rhythm mapping, badminton technique, nature relay challenges, dot painting, ocean-themed problem solving. The activities span physical, creative, cognitive and environmental skills, and most are designed to work better with a group than they would alone.

Blossom Lounge

Ages 13+

Blossom Lounge

In the phase that transitions from childhood to adulthood, hormonal changes and life transitions can lead to heightened emotions and personality revelations. Longer-format sessions ask more of our older children — a kintsugi mindset workshop on resilience, eco house design using recycled materials, memory challenges, strategy-based treasure hunts. Studio space and VR treadmills sit alongside the outdoor programme. Teens tend to be surprised.

Play Spaces

The Buttercup Play Spaces

Buttercup is named for a flower known for its healing properties. Three Buttercup play spaces sit within the Jayela family area and nearby the Jayela family pools.

Buttercup Play Space

Buttercup Play Space

Near the retail village and partially shaded, the Buttercup Play Space is designed to grow with whoever walks into it. Sand and small huts for the very young, slides, seesaws and climbing for the middle years, balance hammocks and taller challenges for older children. The same space — different worlds, depending on the age.

Buttercup Pool Play

Buttercup Pool Play

In the shade of the Jayela Terraces, next to the children's pool. The walls are lined with bouldering grips and the rubberised floor rises into partial domes — the kind of space that takes about 30 seconds to understand and considerably longer to leave.

Buttercup Sand Play

Buttercup Sand Play

Closest to the cove, shaded and unhurried. Climbing, a slide, tunnels, and a direct pathway to the beach — accessible for strollers and mobility equipment. The sea is close enough that it feels like a starting point rather than a destination.

Yasmeen Night Garden

Yasmeen Night Garden

Named for the Arabic word for jasmine, Yasmeen is a quiet setting for evening stargazing. Enter through the moon gate to find reclining loungers angled towards the sky, alongside circular tables for shared observation. Jasmine and gardenia fill the evening air with fragrance.

Family-side facilities

Jayela Family Facilities

Family Pools

Two pools sit at the ground floor of the Jayela Terraces — one for swimming, one shallow enough for the youngest children to stand in. Shaded seating for the adults who are watching, and changing rooms close enough that nobody has to go far.

The Beach Hut

The Beach Hut is where the water begins. Stand-up paddleboard, snorkelling, swim lessons and water-borne yoga — non-motorised by design, so activity on the Red Sea leaves it as it found it. Rinsing showers on site. A path from the Jayela pools to the beach makes access easy for everyone.

Cook together

Nakeha Cooking Studio

Gather around the kitchen to cook, taste and learn together. Guided by our chefs, families explore wholesome ingredients and simple techniques, using fresh produce grown nearby in the Farm Belt whenever possible.

Family hydrothermal circuit

Shared Circuit

Family Hydrothermal

The family hydrothermal circuit is a progressive sequence of hot and cold, wet and steamy experiences designed to be done together. It begins with gentle heat in the infrared sauna, moves through waterfall bathing and Kneipp walking, then into a series of vitality pools at varying temperatures and mineral compositions — warm immersion alternating with cooler contrast to help the body regulate naturally.

Steam, rasul rituals, and a flotation pool deepen the experience as the circuit progresses. Rest and hydration are built in throughout, so the pace is never rushed. The circuit is adaptable for different ages and forms part of the retreat programme.

Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

At what age can children join the adult programme?

Children aged 15 and under are eligible for the children's programme. Older children can enjoy the adult programme; selected activities require parental consent.

Do adult and children's programmes run at the same time?

Yes — programmes for adults and children run in parallel. Your Wellness Advisor will help coordinate and adjust to suit your family.

Is supervision provided throughout children's sessions?

Yes — trained child development teams run structured supervision throughout every session. Parental participation is also encouraged.
