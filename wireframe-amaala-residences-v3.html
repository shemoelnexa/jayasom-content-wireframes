Why these residences

One brief, residences included

One brief runs through every part of Jayasom, residences included. A wellness destination first; the architecture and interiors shaped to support how people live there over a year, not how a brochure photographs in a week. Community, practitioners, and programme all sit alongside the wellness destination. The home is private.

A neighbour to the culture, a neighbour to the locals. The community sits within Saudi geography rather than around it. Architecture choreographs spaces from quiet and reflective to curiosity-provoking and vibrantly social. Studio Carter shaped interiors that express the lyrical essence of the surrounding naturescapes. The residences tell a unique Red Sea story.

Architecture and interiors

By Heah & Co; interiors by Studio Carter

Heah & Co balanced modernism with organic contextual design, prioritising the natural beauty of the setting and instilling a deep connection with the landscape. Studio Carter rooted the interiors in artistic expression and sustainability, crafting a lived well-being experience as immersive and meaningful as it is narratively rich. Handcrafted touches and a softly sculptural aesthetic emphasise fluidity, elegance and a tactile sense of beauty.

Materials draw on the local palette where the climate allows, with low-rise design that recedes against the coastline. Each residence carries pause-object detailing, sea-breeze cross-ventilation before mechanical cooling, and a private plunge pool angled to the horizon.

Investment and ownership

The Jayasom Longevity Assessment Programme

Ownership joins a seven-component annual baseline carried out across the resort's Health and Wellness Centre.

  • An appointed personal Wellness Advisor
  • Blood test analysis and micronutrient analysis once a year
  • Fitness, postural, kinesthetic, skin and biofeedback assessments twice a year
  • Daily activity programme access
  • Complimentary land transfer up to two round trips per year
  • Twenty per cent off treatments and food and beverage
  • Inclusion in the rental programme is optional

Wellness Advisor continuity

The same Wellness Advisor who shapes a retreat stay can shape the residence cycle. Pre-arrival conversations, in-residence consultations and post-departure follow-up sit within one continuous file rather than across separate stays.

Within the master plan

Where the residences sit

Residences sit within the wellness core at the southern end of Triple Bay, connected to Jayasom AMAALA by a linear walking route. The northern end of AMAALA hosts the Marina Village. Residence owners hold standing access to the resort's Health and Wellness Centre, the Yasmeen and Stonecrop gardens at Jayana, and the eight dining venues across the property.

Place is part of the practice. The Red Sea coast holds the year-round outdoor climate; the gardens carry botanical etymology drawn from the regional palette; the kitchens are fed by the on-property Farm Belt. The architecture sits low against the coastline so the place itself does the loudest work.

How many residences are there?

Twenty-four in total. Ten two-bedroom and twelve three-bedroom beach villas, two four-bedroom villas, and a five-bedroom Jayela Residence.

What is the Jayasom Longevity Assessment Programme?

A seven-component owner programme: a personal Wellness Advisor, annual blood and micronutrient analysis, biannual fitness, postural, kinesthetic, skin and biofeedback assessments, daily activity programme access, complimentary land transfers and twenty per cent off treatments and food and beverage.
