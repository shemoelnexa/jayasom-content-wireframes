## Module 3: Description
*Flag: NEW: module*

### H2
What the session is

**Body, paragraph 1 (what it is):**
The session is a controlled mineral immersion. The bath is prepared with mineral concentrate at a temperature and salinity selected for detox aims; the practitioner reads the body before and after, sets a precise duration, and stays within the room. The detoxifying register pairs naturally with adjacent hydrothermal facilities, so a session is often planned alongside salt inhalation or infrared.

**Body, paragraph 2 (why):**
Guests come to Balneotherapy when the body is holding fluid, when circulation is sluggish at the extremities, when a longer detox arc is in motion across the stay. The work is small and constant, the kind that holds up across a year. The change registers as a body that reads less swollen, hands and feet that warm more readily, and a clearer line between stored load and what has cleared.

**Body, paragraph 3 (how it feels):**
Warm mineral water held at a consistent temperature; the body floats with light buoyancy; underwater jets work through back and legs in a programmed sequence. Most guests close their eyes within the first two minutes.

## Module 5: Method
*Flag: NEW: module*

### H2
How the session works

**Body:**
The session draws on Body across the Therapeutic Pillars and sits within the Hydrothermal facilities. The protocol follows three stages: a brief reading of the body and brief, the prepared mineral bath at controlled temperature and salinity for a measured duration, and a slow closing rest. The practitioner stays in the room and reads vitals between intervals, adjusting duration if the body asks for less. The session pairs naturally with adjacent hydrothermal work: salt inhalation rooms, infrared, or a closing cold plunge. The brief read by the Wellness Advisor at arrival sets where Balneotherapy sits across the wider stay.
