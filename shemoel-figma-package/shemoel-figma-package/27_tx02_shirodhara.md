## Module 3: Description
*Flag: NEW: module*

### H2
What Shirodhara is

**Body, paragraph 1 (what it is):**
The guest lies on a wooden Ayurvedic table, eyes covered, hair drawn back. Warm oil, medicated to constitution, flows from a copper vessel suspended above. The pour is steady, the room is quiet, the practitioner moves the stream in slow lateral arcs across the forehead. The session typically runs ninety minutes from settle to close.

**Body, paragraph 2 (why):**
Ayurveda holds that a continuous warm pour at the forehead settles the part of the nervous system that runs on alert. Modern clinical work on the vagus nerve points the same direction. Guests come to Shirodhara when ordinary sleep methods stop working, when migraines are stacking, when the head feels loud.

**Body, paragraph 3 (how it feels):**
Most guests fall asleep inside the first ten minutes. The pour can take a moment to register, then the body recognises it. The room stays warm and dim through the session. Afterwards the head feels lighter, the eyes feel slower, conversation comes harder for an hour.

How the session works

**Body:**
Therapeutic Pillar: Ayurveda, body-mind axis. The oil is selected to constitution at the pre-session brief; sesame oil is the common base, medicated with herbs the practitioner picks for the guest. The pour rate, oil temperature and stream height are calibrated by the practitioner; nothing about the session is automated.

## Module 9: FAQ
*Flag: NEW: module — SEO ADD*

### H2
Common questions

**FAQ Q+A 1:**
Q: What is Shirodhara? A: An Ayurvedic session in which warm medicated oil is poured in a continuous stream over the forehead for around forty minutes, settling the nervous system. The full session including settle and close runs ninety minutes.

**FAQ Q+A 2:**
Q: Is it safe for everyone? A: Pregnant guests, guests with open scalp wounds, and guests on photosensitising medication should brief the Wellness Advisor ahead. The practitioner adapts or routes to a different session where needed.

**FAQ Q+A 3:**
Q: How many sessions before I notice a change? A: Sleep often shifts after one. Migraine work is paced across three to seven sessions; the Ayurvedic practitioner sets the cadence at the brief.

**FAQ Q+A 4:**
Q: What oil is used? A: A sesame oil base medicated to your Ayurvedic constitution; the herbs are picked at the pre-session read. Vegan and nut-free alternatives are available on request.

**FAQ Q+A 5:**
Q: Will my hair be oily afterwards? A: Yes, the oil is part of the work; allow several hours before washing. Guests usually plan it for late afternoon and let the oil sit through dinner.
