## Module 18: Stay structure

**Tier ladder + table:**
Stay length shapes the depth of the work. Four tier names hold the structure: four nights to Explore, seven nights to Absorb, ten nights to Immerse, fourteen nights to Commit. The pacing matches the tier; nothing across the stay is added because of the calendar. Tier table: - Explore: 4 nights, 10 named treatments - Absorb: 7 nights, 18 named treatments - Immerse: 10 nights, 26 named treatments - Commit: 14 nights, 35 named treatments What each tier holds, in plain terms. Explore at four nights resets the rhythm around a short stay or visit. By seven nights, Absorb sees a full habit-cycle take hold. Immerse adds the depth where retreat work begins to compound. The fourteen-night Commit tier is for women wanting the full arc, with practitioner relationships built across the stay. Your Wellness Advisor will recommend a tier during the pre-arrival conversation. CTA: Speak to a Wellness Advisor about the right tier for you

## Module 20: Practitioners in Residence

**Resident-clinicians + 3 consultation pathways + Wellness Advisor + Visiting Practitioners:**
Resident-clinicians hold the medical and clinical layer of the programme. Hormonal and wellness assessments are run by the resident-clinician on day one and revisited at the closing conversation. Diagnostics available to the assessment include blood-panel testing, food-intolerance testing, and Heart-Focused Breathing and Biofeedback (a heart-rate variability protocol that informs nervous-system pacing across the stay). Three named consultation pathways sit alongside the standard assessment: Traditional Arabic and Islamic Medicine (TAIM, drawn from the regional heritage); Naturopathic; and Ayurvedic. Each pathway is matched to where you are and the questions you arrive carrying. The Wellness Advisor holds the programme across the consultation team, from pre-arrival through to closing. Visiting Practitioners rotate in season for specialist tracks: integrative therapists, somatic practitioners, embodied-practice teachers. The roster shifts; check the current rotation at the pre-arrival call. Hands-on therapists run the bodywork tracks. Women-only therapist preferences are honoured

How is the retreat structured around perimenopause vs postnatal? The Wellness Advisor reads where the body is on the day, including life stage and any clinical history shared in the pre-arrival conversation. **FAQ Q+A 4: Are there practitioners specifically for hormonal health?:**
Are there practitioners specifically for hormonal health? Yes. Eman Alsayoud anchors As a Woman, with the in-house Wellness Advisor and Visiting Practitioners reachable across the stay. Women's Hormonal Health is one of three healing-menu programmes that thread across breakfasts, mains and snacks at the property's restaurants, calibrated by the culinary team alongside the clinical work.

**FAQ Q+A 5: Can the retreat flex around my cycle or schedule?:**
Can the retreat flex around my cycle or schedule? Yes. Stays run from four to fourteen nights with any-day arrival. The Wellness Advisor shapes the programme around what the body is asking for on the day, and the cycle is part of that conversation. Pacing eases back where the day asks for less; longer stays carry deeper work where the body has the resources for it.
