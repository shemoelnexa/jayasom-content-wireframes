## Module 3: Description
*Flag: NEW: module*

### H2
What the session is

**Body, paragraph 1 (what it is):**
A private hydrothermal room with a single floatation pod. The water carries roughly five hundred kilograms of dissolved magnesium sulphate, which lifts the body to the surface. Air and water are matched to skin temperature; the boundary between the two becomes hard to find. Guests enter, lie back, and stay.

**Body, paragraph 2 (why):**
Sensory subtraction is the principle: the day's input falls away, the head clears, the breath lengthens on its own. Some guests come for the kind of mental quiet daytime will not deliver; some for sleep that holds across the night; some for a nervous-system reset that asks no effort of them.

**Body, paragraph 3 (how it feels):**
Warm, weightless, dark. The water carries the body; the breath does what it wants to do. Most guests notice the buoyancy first, then the silence.

## Module 9: FAQ
*Flag: NEW: module — SEO ADD*

### H2
Common questions

**FAQ Q+A 1:**
Q: Will I float without trying? A: Yes. The water carries roughly five hundred kilograms of dissolved magnesium sulphate, which lifts the body to the surface without effort. Guests who cannot ordinarily float in a pool float in this room.

**FAQ Q+A 2:**
Q: Is the room enclosed? A: The pod can be opened or closed. Most guests close it for the deeper quiet; some leave it open for the first few minutes and close it once the body has settled.

**FAQ Q+A 3:**
Q: Can I do Floatation if I am pregnant? A: Floatation is generally suitable in the second trimester with a clinician's clearance. The Wellness Advisor will route the brief to the resident clinical team before the session is booked.

**FAQ Q+A 4:**
Q: Will I feel anything after the session? A: Many guests notice the muscles let go and the breath lengthen for several hours. Like the rest of the work at Jayasom, the gain may build across a stay rather than from a single visit.

**FAQ Q+A 5:**
Q: Where does Floatation draw from? A: Hydrotherapy is one of the Therapeutic Pillars at Jayasom; the practice draws on the broader hydrothermal facility set across mineral pools, vitality pools, salt inhalation rooms, and the magnesium float pod.
