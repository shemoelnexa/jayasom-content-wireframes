## Module 3: Description
*Flag: NEW: module*

### H2
What Watsu is

**Body, paragraph 1 (what it is):**
The pool is warmed close to body temperature so the boundary between water and skin softens. The guest wears swimwear and is supported by the practitioner throughout; the head stays above water. The session moves through a sequence of cradled positions, slow rotations and gentle stretches; the body is never asked to hold itself.

**Body, paragraph 2 (why):**
Watsu reaches places that land based bodywork cannot. The water removes the gravity load the spine carries every day. Guests with chronic back pain, post surgical guarding, fibromyalgia, or the kind of nervous system load where stillness feels harder than motion often find the session settles them where dry sessions do not. The work is small and constant, the kind that compounds across a year.

**Body, paragraph 3 (how it feels):**
Most guests stop tracking time inside the first few minutes. The water is warm; the practitioner's hands stay steady; the rhythm is unhurried. Some guests fall briefly asleep mid-session; some cry quietly; some emerge talkative, some emerge silent. The body usually feels longer afterwards.

## Module 9: FAQ
*Flag: NEW: module — SEO ADD*

### H2
Common questions

**FAQ Q+A 1:**
Q: How long does a Watsu session take? A: Fifty minutes in the water plus around ten minutes either side for settle and warm shower; plan ninety minutes from arrival to dressing.

**FAQ Q+A 2:**
Q: Do I need to swim? A: No. The practitioner supports you the whole session; the head stays above water; you do not propel yourself. Guests who do not swim regularly are usually surprised by how easy the session feels.

**FAQ Q+A 3:**
Q: Is it suitable during pregnancy? A: Yes, with adaptations; brief at the Wellness Advisor read so the practitioner can adjust the sequence. The water support is often welcome where land bodywork has become uncomfortable.

**FAQ Q+A 4:**
Q: Can children take Watsu? A: The Brochure confirms the Watsu pool is offered for adults and families; sessions for younger guests are paced and adapted by the practitioner. The Wellness Advisor will route by age and brief.

**FAQ Q+A 5:**
Q: Can my partner and I take it together? A: Paired Watsu sessions run for couples; one partner floats supported while the practitioner cradles the other, then the roles swap. Plan with the Wellness Advisor ahead of arrival.
