## Module 3: Description
*Flag: NEW: module*

### H2
What Marma Point Therapy is

**Body, paragraph 1 (what it is):**
The 107 marma points are mapped across the limbs, torso, neck and head. Each is a confluence of muscle, vein, ligament, bone or joint with what the texts describe as a node of life energy. The practitioner reads where charge has built; the session works those points with circular pressure, warm oil and silence.

**Body, paragraph 2 (why):**
Marma work is used where ordinary massage cannot reach. The points sit at the meeting of the physical and the energetic; activating them is read in Ayurveda as restoring flow rather than treating a muscle. Guests who carry old injuries, post-surgical guarding or long-running emotional charge often find the session reaches what other bodywork has missed.

**Body, paragraph 3 (how it feels):**
The pressure is firm but not deep. The room is quiet; the work is precise rather than flowing. Some points feel sharp on first contact, then soften; some go warm; some bring an unexpected breath. Most guests are quieter for the rest of the day after a Marma session.

## Module 9: FAQ
*Flag: NEW: module — SEO ADD*

### H2
Common questions

**FAQ Q+A 1:**
Q: What is Marma Point Therapy? A: An Ayurvedic session that works 107 mapped energy points across the body using calibrated pressure and warm oil; it is read in Ayurveda as restoring flow along the nadis.

**FAQ Q+A 2:**
Q: Is it the same as acupressure? A: They share the idea of mapped points but the lineage is different. Marma comes from Ayurveda; acupressure comes from Traditional Chinese Medicine. Both are offered at Jayasom; the Wellness Advisor routes by brief.

**FAQ Q+A 3:**
Q: Will it hurt? A: The pressure is firm at some points and soft at others. A few points can feel sharp on first contact, then release. Brief any acute pain at the read so the practitioner adapts.

**FAQ Q+A 4:**
Q: Can I have it during pregnancy? A: Several marma points are not worked in pregnancy. The practitioner adapts the session for prenatal guests; brief at the Wellness Advisor read.

**FAQ Q+A 5:**
Q: How often should I take a session across a stay? A: Two or three over a 7 night stay is common; the practitioner sets the cadence at the brief. The work compounds across sessions.
