## Module 3: Description
*Flag: NEW: module*

### H2
What the ritual is

**Body, paragraph 1 (what it is):**
The ritual is a nervous-system protocol delivered through touch, scent and breath. The vagus nerve, which runs from the base of the skull through the chest and into the gut, regulates the shift between sympathetic alertness and parasympathetic rest. The session works along that pathway with rhythmic strokes, warm essential oils, and breathing patterns that lengthen the exhale.

**Body, paragraph 2 (why):**
Guests come to this session when sleep is broken, when the body is staying braced through the day, when the system reads safe but does not feel it. The work is small and constant, the kind that holds up across a year. The change registers as quieter sleep, slower breath at rest, less guarding through the shoulders and the gut.

**Body, paragraph 3 (how it feels):**
Low light, an unhurried opening, the practitioner working along neck, jaw and shoulders with rhythmic strokes and warm essential oils. Most guests notice the breath lengthening before they notice anything else.

## Module 5: Method
*Flag: NEW: module*

### H2
How the ritual works

**Body:**
The ritual draws on Body and Mind across the Therapeutic Pillars, with breathing protocols informed by the Heart-Focused Breathing and Biofeedback work in the Wellness Assessment programme. The somatic protocol follows three stages: settle the body through grounded touch along the back and feet; open the vagal pathway through neck, chest and abdomen with warm oils; close with paced breathing that lengthens the exhale beyond the inhale. The practitioner adapts intensity and duration to what the body presents that day.

Common questions

It regulates the shift between alert and rested states. The ritual works along that pathway through touch, scent and paced breathing to ease a stress response that has been running too long.

Q: Is this a massage or a clinical session? A: It sits between the two. The touch is therapeutic massage; the protocol draws on nervous-system science and breathing work that the resort's Heart-Focused Breathing and Biofeedback programme uses in its assessments. The work is not diagnostic.
