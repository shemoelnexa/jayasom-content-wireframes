As a Pair Profound connection, relaxing together, renewal. For two people, deeply invested in their togetherness. As a Pair sits within Jayasom AMAALA on Saudi Arabia's Red Sea coast, a wellness destination where the regional healing tradition meets contemporary clinical practice as the working methodology, not as setting. ## Module 14: Your Days Together

**Body:**
Your Days Together No two arrival mornings look the same. Your Wellness Advisor sits down with both of you on day one, reads the questionnaire you completed in the weeks before, and listens to where each partner is on the day. The programme is built from there. The conversation runs longer than a standard arrival intake; what each of you says shapes the week. A typical As a Pair day begins at the studio. Mat Pilates, Morning Yoga Flow, Yin Yoga, or Tai Chi run at seven; you choose by mood. The eleven o'clock Stretch Class is a property anchor and runs every day of the week. Lunch is unhurried and slow-served at Saratô, the three-meal restaurant on the Jayana side, or in lighter form at Chia Bite near the wellness centre; the kitchen minimises gluten, refined sugars, salt, and coffee, and reaches first for what the Farm Belt grows. Afternoons hold the booked treatments. Evenings shift gentler: a Pranayama or Singing Bowl Meditation around six, a slow dinner at Saratô or at Jiraka (the spice-route specialty kitchen, beef-free), and one of the night practices on the calendar (Candlelight Meditation, Better Sleep Class, or Star Gazing) for those staying out longer. Three meals each day from the kitchen, beginning with dinner on arrival. The room is set up around your stated preferences: aroma options of Calming Lavender or Spirited Rosemary, a chosen pillow from the seven on the menu, a Coco-mat™ natural mattress. Pacing is part of the practice. Each day stays open enough to catch what the body and the conversation surface.

## Module 29: Arriving Together

**Day 1 body:**
Day 1: Arriving Together You arrive in the early afternoon. The Jayana Pavilion King is ready by three, set up around the booking-form preferences you sent in: a chosen aroma (Calming Lavender or Spirited Rosemary), a chosen pillow from the seven on the menu, and the Coco-mat™ natural mattress. Welcome amenities sit on the table: fruit juice, tea, water, fruit, snacks. Within the hour your Wellness Advisor sits down with both of you. The questionnaire you completed in the weeks before is already read. The conversation reviews where each partner is on the day and confirms goals from the six directions in the methodology. From there, your Wellness Advisor shapes the programme that begins at the first treatment slot tomorrow. The questions reach further than a standard arrival intake. Dinner is the first meal of the stay. The kitchen serves it slowly at the table, paced through the evening. After dinner, the calendar offers a Singing Bowl Meditation around six. Late-evening practices follow at eight: Candlelight Meditation or a Star Gazing session in the Yasmeen Night Garden. Both stay open invitations on the day.


## Module 30: Finding the Day's Rhythm

**Day 2 body:**
Day 2: Finding the Day's Rhythm The day begins at the studio. The seven o'clock class on Day 2 is whatever the calendar holds for that day of the week: Mat Pilates, Yin Yoga, Tai Chi, or Morning Yoga Flow. Both partners attend; the early hour is the quiet one of the day. The Stretch Class at eleven is a property anchor and runs every day of the week. Lunch is unhurried at one; the kitchen leans toward what the Farm Belt holds and minimises gluten, refined sugars, salt, and coffee. The first booked treatment lands in the afternoon. For most pairs this is the Postural Corrective Therapy session, sixty minutes, run by the physiotherapy team. The work covers physical analysis and corrective movement pacing. What the practitioner finds informs the Wellness Advisor's pacing for the rest of the week. By late afternoon the property quietens. A Pranayama or Singing Bowl Meditation slot at six is open to anyone who wants it. Dinner runs slow again. A Better Sleep Class at eight is available; the daily rhythm builds toward recovery
