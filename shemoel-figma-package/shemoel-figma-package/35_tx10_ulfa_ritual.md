**Body:**
Ūlfa is the Arabic word for closeness and warmth between people. The ritual is built for that: parents and children in the same room, working through approachable sensory experiences together, rooted in generosity and gratitude. The session runs fifty or ninety minutes. The work is shared.

## Module 3: Description
*Flag: NEW: module*

### H2
What the ritual is

**Body, paragraph 1 (what it is):**
A shared sensory ritual for a parent and a child, or a parent and several children, set in a private room at the Family Wellness Centre. The session opens with a slow grounding sequence; moves through scent, touch, and sound at a child-paced rhythm; closes with a quiet gratitude practice that the family carries out together.

**Body, paragraph 2 (why):**
Families come to Ūlfa for the kind of attention that does not require performance. The work is small and constant, the kind that holds up across a stay. Most parents notice the room before the children do; most children notice the warmth before they notice anything else.

**Body, paragraph 3 (how it feels):**
Warm, slow, shared. The practitioner sets the pace to whatever the youngest in the room can hold; children usually settle within the first ten minutes, sometimes before the adults do.

## Module 5: Method
*Flag: NEW: module*

### H2
How the ritual works

**Body:**
The ritual draws on Arabic healing methods, one of the categories on the property's Wellness Wisdom shelf. The sensory layers are scaled to age: a younger child receives the same structure at a softer register; a teenager receives the same structure at a more spoken register. The pace is set by the youngest in the room. The pressure is light throughout.
