## Module 20: Residences density backfill
*Flag: NEW: append element*

**Body paragraph (24-residence portfolio canonical):**
Within Triple Bay alongside Jayasom AMAALA, twenty-four wellness residences. Three layouts hold the portfolio: ten two-bedroom residences at three hundred and fifty square metres, twelve three-bedroom residences at four hundred and twenty-three square metres, and two four-bedroom residences at five hundred and twenty-five square metres. Each layout faces the Red Sea on the southern end of the master plan.

**Body paragraph (architecture and interiors):**
The architecture is by Heah and Co; the interiors are by Studio Carter. Materials draw on the local palette where the climate allows, with low-rise design that recedes against the coastline. Each residence carries pause-object detailing, sea-breeze cross-ventilation before mechanical cooling, and a private plunge pool angled to the horizon.

**Body paragraph (Owner Benefits and Longevity Assessment):**
Owners receive the Jayasom Longevity Assessment Programme, a seven-component annual baseline carried out across the resort's Health and Wellness Centre. Cardiovascular, metabolic, hormonal, neurocognitive, gut, sleep and movement markers run on a calibrated cadence, with results read by the Wellness Advisor and the Director of Health and Wellness. The work compounds across years.

**Body paragraph (rental programme and yield framing):**
An optional rental programme runs through the resort operator. Owners place residences into the rental pool when not in residence, with operator-managed turnover, housekeeping and guest experience. {TBC}

**Body paragraph (community and master plan placement):**
Residences sit within the wellness core at the southern end of Triple Bay, connected to Jayasom AMAALA by a linear walking route. The northern end of AMAALA hosts the Marina Village. Residence owners hold standing access to the resort's Health and Wellness Centre, the Yasmeen and Stonecrop gardens at Jayana, and the eight dining venues across the property.

**Body paragraph (Wellness Advisor continuity):**
The same Wellness Advisor who shapes a retreat stay can shape the residence cycle. Owners arrive into a known relationship; the body of work built across earlier retreats threads into the longer cadence. Pre-arrival conversations, in-residence consultations and post-departure follow-up sit within one continuous file rather than across separate stays.

**Body paragraph (place anchor):**
Place is part of the practice. The Red Sea coast holds the year-round outdoor climate; the gardens carry botanical etymology drawn from the regional palette; the kitchens are fed by the on-property Farm Belt. The architecture sits low against the coastline so the place itself does the loudest work. Nawaf Bashmail's framing applies: a neighbour to the culture, a neighbour to the locals.
