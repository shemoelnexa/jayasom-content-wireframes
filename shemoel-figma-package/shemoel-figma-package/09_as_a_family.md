## Module 13: Approach

**How the family programme works (5 paragraphs):**
A family-attachment retreat with concurrent programmes for parents and young people, designed to nurture the relationships that matter most. Time held to fortify further as a family, in love, trust, and mutual respect. Each member is guided to recognise unique strengths, fostering improved communication, teamwork, and accountability. The work moves through enriching group and individual experiences in a fun and relaxed environment. The programme blends Family Wellness mentoring with mind-body practices, fitness training, and physiotherapy, alongside shared activities including nature experiences, cooking classes, Spa and restorative rituals, and play-based fitness for children.

## Module 27: Stay structure

**5-tier ladder + Family treatment counts + adult-child duality:**
Family treatment counts scale with stay length. Explore at four nights gives eleven family treatments. Absorb at seven nights gives eighteen. Immerse at ten nights gives twenty-four. Commit at fourteen nights gives thirty-four. Across the tiers, the daily average sits around two and a half family treatments a day; the longer the stay, the more the rhythm settles into a per-day cadence rather than a per-tier total.


## Module 28: Activities by Day

**5-slot 7-day rotation + 30 Skill Development tags:**
The children's day runs to a five-slot rhythm across a seven-day rotation. Each slot holds a single activity, calibrated to the age band, 40 minutes long. - 9:00 to 10:00, first morning slot, mixed across age bands. - 10:00 to 11:00, second morning slot, mixed across age bands. - 15:00 to 16:00, afternoon slot, mixed across age bands. - 16:00 to 17:00, the family hour, every day. Mindful Balance Relay, We Are Connected, Stories from Home, Circle of Trust, Family Rhythm Ritual, Guess Who, and Family Mini Olympics rotate across the seven days. - 17:00 to 18:00, evening slot, holding the teen tier most days, with a Saturday infant Sensory Hand & Arm Painting wind-down for the youngest band. Activities across the rotation are tagged across thirty Skill Development categories, including emotional development, sensory awareness, cognitive skills, fine and gross motor skills, language development, family connection, problem solving, and environmental awareness. Children rotate through the bands and through the categories across a seven-night stay, longer where the tier permits. Universal duration for every children's activity: 40 minutes. Five daily slots return roughly three hours and twenty minutes of structured time per child per day, with the family hour cross-counted

## Module 30: Recommended Add-ons for As a Family

Three add-on programmes are specifically recommended for As a Family stays, drawn from the JSAMS Health & Wellness Retreats Advisors Course.
