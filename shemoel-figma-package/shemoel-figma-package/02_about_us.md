## Module 4: Founding Story
*Flag: NEW: module*

### H2
How Jayasom began

**Body (3 paragraphs, ~280 words):**
Jayasom began in 2014 with a single purpose. The brand exists to inspire and curate wellness, in places designed so guests can live the practice rather than perform it. Western evidence-based methods sit alongside holistic remedies and local healing traditions, and programmes are paced rather than prescribed. The property at AMAALA on Saudi Arabia's Red Sea coast, built in partnership with Red Sea Global, is where that purpose has its physical home. Smaller decisions, the sort that go unnoticed in larger properties, are the ones that carry the experience here. The Yasmeen Night Garden takes its name from the Arabic word for jasmine, the flower that opens only after dark. The planting answers to that rhythm, so the garden carries a different scent in the hours after sunset. Across parts of the property the flower beds are planted to chakra colour schemes, each bed worked through a single colour from root to crown. The Farm Belt grows bananas, pomegranates, herbs, broccoli, and salad leaves on the property; signs in the beds name what each row supports on the day's menu. Kitchens minimise gluten, refined sugars, salt, and coffee, on the principle that flavour comes from herbs and technique instead. The dining studio's research on what foods support what conditions runs continuously, and the menu adjusts as the assessments and the seasons move. Every choice earns its place. The same brief runs through every part of the property. The kitchens reach first for what the Farm Belt grows. Sourcing decisions are checked against measurements kept at the Mosa Hub. The morning's assessment shapes that day's programme. The work runs together as one practice

## Module 8: Place: AMAALA at Triple Bay
*Flag: NEW: module*

### H2
Where we are

**Body (4 paragraphs, ~175 words):**
Jayasom sits at AMAALA on Saudi Arabia's Red Sea coast, within the Triple Bay master plan being developed by Red Sea Global. AMAALA is one of the most ambitious wellness-anchored regenerative tourism destinations under construction anywhere in the world. The location matters to the work. Deep traditions of healing and hospitality run along the Red Sea coast, long pre-dating modern wellness. The light here, the salt air, the temperature swing between day and shore-evening, all play a part in how the body settles. The team has spent time learning from local healers and midwives whose practice is inherited rather than packaged. Jayasom's part of the master plan covers 60 wellness-resort villas and 24 branded residences, with eight dining venues, 76 treatment rooms, 34 studios, and 16 consultation rooms across the property. Jayasom sits one hour by car from Al Wajh airport. Red Sea International is two hours away, and NEOM Bay is also two hours. What is built for guests carries this character into every part of the stay

## Module 10: Partners + Architects
*Flag: NEW: module*

### H2
Who built it

**Body (3 paragraphs, ~135 words):**
Jayasom is built in partnership with Red Sea Global, the master developer of AMAALA, whose vision for regenerative tourism gave the project its ground. Heah & Co drew the architecture. A practice with deep work across destination resorts, they were briefed to make the buildings recede in the right places: low against the coastline, oriented to the wadi behind, set back from the sea so that the water remains the foreground. Studio Carter handled the interiors, with a calibrated use of natural materials, pause-object detailing, and rooms that feel quietly held rather than branded. Together, the design partners and the wellness team have built a wellness destination that wears its considered work lightly. Nothing here is for show. The work holds up to inspection
