Retreat Finder

Find the retreat that matches what you came in for.

Six retreats. One question first. What is asking for attention right now?

The retreat that fits is not always the one that catches the eye first. A few short questions, the same ones a Wellness Advisor would open with, point you toward the retreat the body and mind are asking for. The shape of the stay is built around you on arrival, after a clinical functional assessment. This page is the door.

Start the questions Speak to a Wellness Advisor

Step one

What is asking for attention?

Pick what feels closest. You can choose more than one. The questions move with you, and the retreat results adjust as the picture sharpens. Nothing here commits you. The Wellness Advisor will refine the shape on arrival.

Sleep Stress Energy Connection Weight Fatigue Digestion Hormonal change

Tell us a little more. Has it been weeks, or longer? What does it feel like in the day? You won't be locked in by what you say here.

Step two

Six retreats. One that fits.

One retreat is highlighted as the closest match to what you said. Two more sit alongside as adjacent fits. Each is offered in 4, 7, 10, or 14-night durations and shaped at arrival by your Wellness Advisor.

Karen Campbell on the approach

Karen Campbell calls this the ingredients-in-the-kitchen approach. The kitchen is the property: Therapeutic Pillars, named practitioners, treatments, gardens, dining venues. The felt-need is what you came to cook. Your Wellness Advisor walks you through how to combine the two.

Questions

Common questions before booking

How does Find Your Retreat work?

You answer a short series of questions about what is asking for attention right now: sleep, stress, energy, connection, weight, fatigue, digestion, hormonal change, or something else. The system surfaces one retreat as the closest match, two more as adjacent, and three as further afield. You read the cards. You speak to a Wellness Advisor before booking if it helps.

Can I choose more than one felt-need?

Yes. The questions are designed for that. Most guests arrive with two or three things layered together; the matching takes that into account.

What is the minimum stay?

Retreats run from four nights upward. The most common stays are seven, ten, and fourteen nights. Per-tier inclusions vary; longer stays add more practitioner time and deeper assessment.

Do I have to know what I want?

No. If the picture is still forming, request a call with a Wellness Advisor. Many guests arrive with a sense that something needs to shift, without yet knowing what. The pre-arrival call shapes the week around what each guest says.

What is AMAALA?
