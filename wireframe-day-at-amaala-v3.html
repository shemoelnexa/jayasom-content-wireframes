AMAALA · A Day in the Life

A Day at AMAALA

What it feels like to spend a day inside the wellness core.

Your day shapes itself. Mornings open with movement; the rest follows from how you arrive. The schedule shifts to suit you, not the other way around. The team brings what's needed.

Plan a Day

01 · Morning

Movement opens the day

You wake to the cove or to the wadi behind your villa. Coffee or matcha at Saratô, or a quiet breakfast on the terrace. Your first session might be yoga at the open studio, breathwork on the Wellness Plateau, or hydrotherapy in the lower pools. The Wellness Advisor sets the order.

Sunrise on the cove
Treatment room, garden-facing

02 · Mid-Morning

Treatments through the morning

Treatments run through the morning. Watsu in the warm pool, sound healing in the gardens, or a bodywork session in a treatment room that opens onto its own enclosed garden. The pace is yours. Long sessions or short.

03 · Lunch

A slow lunch on the cove

Saratô on the cove side runs a slow lunch. Loumi Rue holds the family side. Both kitchens cook for restoration: no alcohol, no deep frying, fourteen approved methods, and produce drawn from the Farm Belt that runs the length of the property.

Saratô · cove side

Loumi Rue · family side

04 · Afternoon

Quiet hours hold

Quiet hours hold from after lunch. Rest in the villa, or walk the Wellness Plateau to the Stonecrop Maze. Pause at the centre; retrace your steps. Stonecrop is a succulent that holds water; it associates with peace, perseverance, and calmness.

A Story Inside the Day

At Loumi Rue, a story

The black lemon, born into a family of citrus. Different. Misunderstood. Then a friendship with parsley, who carries the same confusion. The two of them found a friendship that comes from being misunderstood. The family restaurant sits inside that story.

05 · Late Afternoon

Movement returns

Movement returns. Pilates, functional training, water-borne yoga at the Beach Hut, or a class at Nakeha Studio with the chefs. The four o'clock to five o'clock slot holds something for everyone in the property; this is when the Family side and the adult side most often meet.

Yasmeen Night Garden

06 · Evening

Slow dinner, reduced light

Dinner is slow tonight, longer than the others. Saratô, or Chez Mitzuba on the family side. Then the Yasmeen Night Garden, where lighting is reduced and the moon and the stars come into view. Phoenix Garden tables hold longer conversations under the palms.

Frequently Asked Questions

How the day works

Does the schedule run on fixed times?

No. The Wellness Advisor sets the order each morning around what your body asks for, the practitioners on hand that week, and the weather. Loose anchors hold; the rest flexes.

Can families and adult guests share a day?

Mostly the two sides hold their own pace, with a daily four o'clock to five o'clock slot where adults and children most often meet. Evenings flex around dinner at Saratô or Loumi Rue.
