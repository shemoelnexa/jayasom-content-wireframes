Sound Healing

Energy and sound for all ages, in a quiet room.

Tibetan singing bowls, gongs, and toned voice carry the work. Guests lie still; the practitioner moves around the room. The body listens before the mind does, and the nervous system slows in response. The session lasts fifty minutes and sits within the wider practice at Jayasom.

Plan a session
Singing bowls and gongs in the studio

Description

What the session is

A guided exposure to vibration. The practitioner works with calibrated singing bowls, gong, tuning forks, and toned voice, sometimes layered, sometimes singular. Guests lie supine on a mat or recline in a chair; nothing is asked of the body other than to receive.

Across a stay, the work is small and constant. Some guests come for sleep that holds; some for the quiet long tones leave behind; some for a nervous system that loosens its grip after weeks at speed.

Long tones that hold, short tones that pass through. The body settles before the mind does. Silence holds for a long beat after the last tone, then the hour closes.

From the practice

"The body listens before the mind does."

Method

How the session works

The session draws on the Energy and Sound Therapeutic Pillar. The work is non-contact and non-verbal. Suitable for guests of all ages, including children when paired with a guardian.

Tibetan singing bowls

Primary instruments. Long tones that hold, short tones that pass through.

Gongs

Wider register, deeper sustain. Worked alongside the bowls.

Tuning forks & toned voice

Extend the register; layered or singular as the practitioner reads the room.

Outcomes

What guests notice afterwards

  • A nervous system that settles without effort
  • A breath that lengthens and holds
  • A way back into the body when the day has been spent in the head
  • The kind of quiet that does not require effort to find

Common questions

Frequently asked

Is Sound Healing suitable for children?

Yes. Energy and sound healing is offered for all ages, and a family-adapted version of the session sits in the As a Family inclusions catalogue. Children attend with a guardian.

Do I need to do anything during the session?

Lie still and listen. The work is non-contact and non-verbal. Guests are not asked to focus, breathe in a particular way, or follow an instruction; the room does the work.

Is Sound Healing the same as Sound Bath Meditation?

They sit alongside each other in the Mindfulness category. Sound Bath Meditation is led with a guided meditation thread; Sound Healing carries the work through the instruments alone. Both run fifty minutes.

Can Sound Healing help with sleep?

Many guests notice their sleep change after a session. Like the rest of the work at Jayasom, the gain may build across a stay rather than from a single visit.

Where does Sound Healing draw from?

Energy and Sound is one of the Therapeutic Pillars at Jayasom. The instruments draw on Tibetan and Himalayan vibrational traditions; the integration with the wider practice is led by the resident clinical team.
