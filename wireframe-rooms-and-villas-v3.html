Rooms and Villas

A home that breathes to the rhythms of the Red Sea.

Six configurations, each shaped to a different way of staying. The villas and residences sit privately spaced amid lush gardens directly on the beach, with the same architecture, the same brief, the same view. The bedrooms differ; so does the way each home holds a week.

Sensory focus

A sense of place

Open, light-filled spaces emphasise simplicity and serenity. Indigenous materials and exceptional local craftsmanship carry a distinct sense of place. Spacious yet cocooning interiors express the lyrical essence of the surrounding naturescapes. The residences tell a unique Red Sea story.

Zones

Two arrivals, one property

Jayana · adults

South entrance

Jayana

Adult guests arrive at Jayana to the south. The adult retreat side runs the deeper retreat practice and the dinner-led restaurant: Yasmeen Night Garden, Stonecrop Maze, Phoenix Garden, Saratô, Jiraka, and the adult Health and Wellness Centre.

Jayela · families

North entrance

Jayela

Families arrive at Jayela to the north, with the Family Wellness Centre, the four Growing Studios, Loumi Rue, Chez Mitzuba, Breezy Kale, and the family pools. Two distinct entrances open into one Health and Wellness Centre, which sits between them. Practitioners are shared. The programme is shared.

Across the property

Sixty villas plus twenty-four residences

Sixty villas hold the resort across four core layouts: One-Bedroom Beachfront Pool Villa at sixty-two square metres indoor and forty-eight square metres outdoor; Two-Bedroom Beachfront Pool Villa for guests travelling together; Three-Bedroom Pool Villa for cohort or family stays; and Over-Water Villas facing the bay for guests on longer stays. Each layout carries a balcony, lounge chairs, plunge pool, Coco-mat natural mattress, and Oud-custom amenities.

Each room is set up around stated preferences before arrival. Aroma options: Calming Lavender or Spirited Rosemary. Pillow choice from the seven on the menu. The room held to the property's temperature standard. Coco-mat natural mattress: latex, coconut fibre, horse hair, cactus fibre, seaweed, knitted cotton.

Twenty-four wellness residences sit alongside the resort villas across three layouts: ten two-bedroom at three hundred and fifty square metres, twelve three-bedroom at four hundred and twenty-three square metres, and two four-bedroom at five hundred and twenty-five square metres. Architecture is by Heah and Co; interiors by Studio Carter. A five-bedroom Jayela Residence with private wellness studio sits as a separate flagship layout.

Place is a method, not a backdrop. The villa layouts hold the practice as much as the studios do. Phase one is a small property by destination-resort scale, and the design is for it to stay that way. The daily wellness rhythm is sized to the cohort, not to the building.

Questions

Frequently asked questions

How many villa types are there?

Six configurations: two-bedroom, three-bedroom, four-bedroom, the five-bedroom Jayela Residence, the Beach Villa, and the Oceanfront Suite.

What is the difference between rental and ownership?

Rental is a stay through the resort booking. Ownership joins the Jayasom Longevity Assessment Programme; the residence can be included in the rental programme when owners are not in residence.

Where are the villas located?

At Jayasom Wellness Resort, part of Triple Bay AMAALA on the Red Sea coast of Saudi Arabia. Al Wajh airport is one hour by car.
