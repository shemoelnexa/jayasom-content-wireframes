Detoxifying Balneotherapy

Mineral bathing, paced for the detox arc.

Balneotherapy is mineral bathing in a controlled hydrothermal protocol. The thirty-minute session uses warm mineral water under a measured temperature and duration to draw out fluid retention, ease the load on the lymphatic system, and open the body's clearance pathways. The work sits within Jayasom's Hydrothermal facilities, alongside hammam, steam, sauna and salt inhalation.

Overview

What the session is

What it is

A controlled mineral immersion

The session is a controlled mineral immersion. The bath is prepared with mineral concentrate at a temperature and salinity selected for detox aims; the practitioner reads the body before and after, sets a precise duration, and stays within the room. The detoxifying register pairs naturally with adjacent hydrothermal facilities, so a session is often planned alongside salt inhalation or infrared.

Why guests choose it

When the body is holding fluid

Guests come to Balneotherapy when the body is holding fluid, when circulation is sluggish at the extremities, when a longer detox arc is in motion across the stay. The work is small and constant, the kind that holds up across a year. The change registers as a body that reads less swollen, hands and feet that warm more readily, and a clearer line between stored load and what has cleared.

How it feels

Warm, suspended, paced

Warm mineral water held at a consistent temperature; the body floats with light buoyancy; underwater jets work through back and legs in a programmed sequence. Most guests close their eyes within the first two minutes.

Method

How the session works

The session draws on Body across the Therapeutic Pillars and sits within the Hydrothermal facilities. The protocol follows three stages.

Stage 01 · Read

Body and brief

A brief reading of the body and brief; the Wellness Advisor's notes on the wider stay set where Balneotherapy sits.

Stage 02 · Immerse

Prepared mineral bath

The prepared mineral bath at controlled temperature and salinity for a measured duration. The practitioner stays in the room and reads vitals between intervals.

Stage 03 · Rest

Slow closing rest

A slow closing rest. The session pairs naturally with adjacent hydrothermal work: salt inhalation rooms, infrared, or a closing cold plunge.

Common questions

Frequently asked

What does balneotherapy mean?

Balneotherapy is the practice of bathing in mineral water for therapeutic aim. The Detoxifying protocol at Jayasom uses controlled mineral concentration, temperature and duration to support the body's clearance pathways. The work sits within the resort's Hydrothermal facilities.

Is the session safe?

The bath is prepared by a hydrothermal practitioner who stays in the room, reads vitals between intervals, and adjusts duration where the body asks for less. The session is held back where contraindications surface in the pre-session brief; the Wellness Advisor reviews medical history ahead of placement.

Should I plan it alongside other hydrothermal work?

Yes, often. The Hydrothermal facilities at Jayasom are designed to read as one practice. A Balneotherapy session pairs naturally with salt inhalation rooms, infrared, hammam, or a closing cool plunge. The Wellness Advisor sequences these together where the brief calls for an extended hydrothermal arc.

How is this different from a regular spa bath?

The water composition, temperature and duration are selected for a specific detox aim, not a generic relaxation register. The practitioner stays in the room, reads vitals, and adjusts the protocol. The work is read into a wider stay through the Wellness Advisor brief, not booked as a stand-alone indulgence.

Where it is delivered

Jayasom Wellness Resort sits at AMAALA, on Saudi Arabia's Red Sea coast at Triple Bay. Detoxifying Balneotherapy is delivered on-property within the Hydrothermal facilities; one hour by car from Al Wajh airport.
